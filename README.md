# Disaster Resource Exchange Platform (DREP)

A centralized coordination platform for disaster response.

## Problem Statement

During disasters, the challenge is often not the absence of resources, but the inability to discover and coordinate them efficiently. Emergency response involves multiple independent organizations—police, fire departments, hospitals, NGOs, volunteer groups, and government agencies—each managing their own resources separately. This fragmentation makes cross-agency coordination slow, relying on phone calls, emails, and manual follow-ups.

Citizens have no unified way to communicate non-emergency resource needs, and agencies have no shared view of available resources across organizations.

## Our Approach

DREP is a **Smart Disaster Resource Exchange Platform** that enables verified disaster-response organizations to publish, discover, request, and allocate resources through a shared operational network. Instead of replacing existing systems, it serves as an **interoperability layer** connecting participating organizations with a unified view of available resources.

## Expected Impact

- Reduce time to locate critical resources
- Improve cross-agency collaboration
- Increase utilization of existing disaster-response resources
- Reduce duplicate requests and manual coordination
- Provide citizens with a transparent pathway for requesting assistance
